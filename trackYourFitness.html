Body Mass Index (BMI)

What is BMI?

Body mass index (BMI) is a medical screening tool that estimates body fat percentage by using an adult's weight and height. BMI can be calculated using metric or imperial (US) units. In metric units, the formula is weight (kilograms) divided by height squared (meters), or BMI = kg/m².

BMI Category
<16 Severely Underweight
16-18.4 Underweight
18.5-24.9 Normal
25-29.9 Overweight
30-34.9 Moderately Obese
35-39.9 Severely Obese
≥40 Morbidly Obese

Body Fat Percentage(BFP)

What is BFP?

Body Fat Percentage(BFP) is a more accurate way to measure health and disease risk than body mass index (BMI), which only considers overall weight. BMI can't differentiate between fat storage, lean body mass, and muscle mass.
